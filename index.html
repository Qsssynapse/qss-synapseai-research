Background & Context

My work focuses on applied, system-level cybersecurity research, combining AI-assisted security operations with post-quantum transition readiness.

Rather than pursuing theoretical cryptography or quantum hardware, I design deployable security architectures that help enterprises and governments prepare for emerging threats, including those introduced by future quantum capabilities.

I am the founder and system architect of QSS SynapseAI™, an integrated cybersecurity platform designed as a single coherent system, not a collection of disconnected tools.

Research & Engineering Focus

AI-Assisted Detection & Response: Applying machine learning and correlation logic to support threat hunting, anomaly detection, and assisted response in SOC environments.

Post-Quantum Readiness: Evaluating and integrating post-quantum cryptography concepts (Kyber, Dilithium) at architecture and workflow levels, focusing on crypto-agility and migration planning.

Unified Security Architecture: Designing platforms that integrate detection, response, compliance, governance, identity, and infrastructure security into a single operational model.

Security Governance & Automation: Embedding auditability, policy enforcement, and AI governance directly into security operations.

Platform Architecture

QSS SynapseAI™ is structured as a 16-module integrated security suite, developed as one platform architecture with coherent integration between components.

My contribution lies in system design, integration logic, and applied security engineering, translating research concepts into coherent, auditable, and scalable architectures.

Key Differentiators: Applied vs theoretical focus, integration over isolation, practical post-quantum readiness, and AI governance emphasis.

Relevance to Australia

This work aligns with Australia's long-term priorities in:

Cybersecurity Resilience: Critical infrastructure protection and sovereign capability development.

Quantum Strategy Alignment: Supporting Australia's National Quantum Strategy with practical transition planning.

AI Adoption & Governance: Developing practical AI applications for security operations with built-in governance.

Research-Industry Bridge: Partnering with Australian institutions on applied cybersecurity research and skills development.

Goal: Collaborate with Australian researchers on practical quantum-AI security readiness, focusing on applied research, validation, and skills development.
